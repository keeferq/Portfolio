Heatwaves and Gun Violence Analysis

Exploring the Correlation Between Extreme Heat Events and Violent Crime

Project Maps & Visuals

Project Overview

This research project investigated the potential relationship between periods of extreme heat (heatwaves) and the frequency or severity of gun violence incidents across the United States. The project involved acquiring, cleaning, integrating, and analyzing large datasets related to weather patterns and crime reports. Data was processed primarily using R and visualized spatially using web mapping technologies (Leaflet.js) to create interactive explorations.

A key outcome was an interactive web map showcasing processed incident data, alongside static maps highlighting specific findings. The research associated with this project, particularly the poster presentation, received the Best Undergraduate Poster at the Office of Student Research Poster Competetition on April 16, 2025.

Lessons Learned & Key Findings

This project provided valuable experience in handling large, datasets (weather, crime) and developing methods for spatio-temporal analysis. Key findings indicated that the heatwaves and gunviolence has spatial correlation in some ares of the West Coast.

  • Developed proficiency in R for data manipulation (dplyr, tidyr), spatial analysis using sf and time series analysis related to environmental and social data.
  • Gained experience integrating diverse data sources and addressing challenges like temporal/spatial alignment and varying reporting standards.
  • Enhanced skills in creating interactive web maps with Leaflet.js for visualizing complex spatio-temporal patterns effectively.
  • Practiced communicating research findings visually through both static maps and a formal research poster.
  • Future Consideration: Incorporating additional socio-economic variables to explore confounding factors or differential impacts.
