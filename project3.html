Project Description

As an Undergraduate Research Assistant working with Dr. Maggie Sugg, my primary responsibility on this project was the meticulous management and correction of a comprehensive dataset detailing maternal health providers across the United States. A significant portion of this work involved manually verifying and updating the locations of thousands of providers whose addresses failed automated geocoding processes within ArcGIS Pro.

This task required extensive use of the ArcGIS Pro attribute table, selection queries, and external tools (like web searches and provider directories) to accurately locate facilities. While repetitive, this process provided deep familiarity with attribute data management, quality control procedures, and the challenges of working with real-world address data in GIS. The validated dataset was then used in further spatial analyses and visualizations, such as the StoryMap presented above.

Lessons Learned

This assistantship provided practical experience in crucial data management aspects often encountered in research settings.

  • Developed high proficiency in utilizing the ArcGIS Pro attribute table, including complex queries, field calculations, and editing workflows.
  • Mastered manual geocoding and address correction techniques, understanding common pitfalls in automated geocoding.
  • Gained an appreciation for data quality control and the importance of metadata documentation in large research datasets.
  • Enhanced attention to detail and systematic approaches required for handling large volumes of data entries.
  • Understood the collaborative nature of research data management, working alongside graduate students and faculty to produce reliable data for analysis and communication (e.g., StoryMaps).
